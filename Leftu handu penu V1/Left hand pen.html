Franklin Christoph Model 66 Artic Ice Blue

The model of the pen I have got is the Artic Ice Blue Version, exclusive to Singapore. The artic ice blue version shows a light blue under normal and high light conditions with colours akin to a Dasani mineral water bottle. However, when I placed the pen in the pouch it came in , the pen glows to a deep, saturated electric blue that is just aesthetically pleasing to me.


Overall, the pen has a elongated shape that is unique. It looks like a barrel of the syringe, while tapering to the end of the pen, while the thickest part of the pen is near the back of the pen. The body of the pen has a flat side that is used as a roll stopper. It is also faintly engraved with the words "Franklin-Christoph Model 66" The cap of the pen is clipless, with trapezium cylinder shape that tapers to the top of the pen. The pen has flat tops on both ends of the pen. The flat top on the pen is engraved with the Franklin-Christoph logo very faintly and has a certain texture to it that makes it pleasant to fidget with, at least for me.


The pen comes with a #6 size jowo nib. For mine, I got the SIG EF nib. SIG nib, or Stub-Italic Gradient nib, is a Franklin-Christoph exclusive nib that is ground by Jim Rouse. Ever since his passing, his speciality SIG nib has been raffled by Franklin-Christoph on their website. Now, there is a new batch of SIG nibs that are groun by Audrey Matteson. The SIG nib that I got is grounded by her, and it is very smooth (See the Writing profile below). Since I do not have any point of comparisons between the SIG nib ground by both of them.
